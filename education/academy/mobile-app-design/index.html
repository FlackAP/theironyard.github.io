Mobile App Design for iOS

What you will learn and do

Our world is quickly becoming a place where people’s interaction with the web (and each other) happens primarily on mobile devices. At the end of 2012, there were 1.2 billion people using mobile apps, and that number is forecast to grow at almost 30% per year.

We want to help designers navigate this exploding landscape by teaching the fundamentals of building user-centric apps and interfaces that cut through the noise.

Curriculum Overview

Over the course of three night classes, we’ll walk through the process of designing a beautiful iOS app, simplifying interactions, and adding personality to your design to make it stand out from the rest. Here’s what you’ll get from the course:

  • Discuss inspiration vs copying and how to find ideas all around you (not just from other designs)
  • Create a visual style with colors, fonts, patterns and textures
  • Understand the subtle nuances of other great apps
  • Learn the lay of the land—what are some common design conventions?
  • Learn the unique nature of designing for mobile versus desktop
  • A custom app walkthrough—we’ll look at each step in the process of designing a great app
  • Learn about prototyping tools you can use on your phone to test apps as you design them
  • You’ll follow along with each step to design your own app
  • Selected student projects will be posted on The Iron Yard’s website!

Excited? You’ve come to the right place.

Is this for you?

If you’re looking to move from web design into app design, OR you’re a developer looking to build a better interface for your app, this is the class for you.

Are there prerequisite skills?

In order to get the most value from this class, you should have a solid understanding of Photoshop. Ideally this includes layer effects and working with vector objects. You should also have some experience designing or developing for the web/screen.

It’s not required, but we strongly suggest that you bring an iPhone or iPod touch to class.
